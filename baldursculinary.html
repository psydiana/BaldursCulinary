How to make bolinho de chuva

Welcome to a new issue elaborated by our cuisine's finest experts! This time around, we took a trip to the southern hemisphere and ventured through the colorfully vibrant Brazil. There we discovered a recipe for a delicacy called "bolinho de chuva" (little rain cake, literally translating), a sweet, delicious meal perfect for cheering people up on a rainy day, as the name suggests. Stay tuned for the ingredients and instructions on how to make a few for yourself or loved ones!

Ingredients:

  • 2 (two) eggs;
  • 1/2 (half) a teacup of sugar;
  • 1 (one) teacup of milk in room temperature;
  • 2 (two) teacups and 1/2 (a half) of wheat flour;
  • 1 (one) tablespoon of baking powder;
  • Frying oil;
  • To finish the recipe off, 1 (one) teacup of sugar and 1 (one) teaspoon of cinnamon powder.

*Teacup used: 240 ml

*Tip: Always fry with a mid-level fire to ensure it does not stay raw on the inside.

Preperation:

  1. In a bowl, add the 2 eggs, 1/2 teacup of sugar, and a teacup of milk;
  2. Mix the ingredients added for one minute;
  3. Then add 2 teacups and 1/2 of wheat flour, mixing it again;
  4. Afterward add 1 tablespoon of baking powder, mixing the ingredients once again until the dough gains a consistent texture, not too soft nor too hard;
  5. Prepare a frying pan and heat the frying oil at mid-level, avoiding boiling temperature;
  6. With a tablespoon, scoop the dough into ball-like shapes and dip them into the heated frying oil;
  7. In case the little rain cakes don't turn themselves over, give them a little nudge to make sure they're a dark gold color on all sides;
  8. After the dough is fried, scoop some with a spatula and let the excess oil drip down back into the frying pan, then placing them in a container of your choice that has 1 teacup of sugar and 1 teaspoon of cinnamon foiling its bottom, mixing everything together to coat the bolinhos de chuva;
  9. And now you enjoy your fresh meal!
